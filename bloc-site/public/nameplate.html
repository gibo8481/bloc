Nameplate

Download PDF Template

Time: 30 minutes

Level: 1

Supplies

  • Scrap cardboard
  • 1/8 in. x 4 in. x 6 in. plywood
  • Engraving tool
  • Paint or markers
  • Sandpaper
1

Draw Your Design

Start by sketching out your nameplate design. You can use the provided template or create your own design. Include your name or any text you'd like to engrave. Ensure the design fits within the dimensions of the plywood.

2

Secure Materials

Open the lid on your bloc enclosure. Place the sacrificial material (cardboard) on the aluminum bed, then place the plywood on top. Use clamps to secure the materials in place, ensuring they do not move during engraving or cutting. Close the lid.

3

Engrave and Cut

Using the bloc console, select the travel mode and align the spindle with the starting point of your design. Switch to cut mode and turn on the spindle. Lower the Z Axis Joystick to engrave the text or design onto the plywood.

Once the engraving is complete, adjust the spindle to cut out the shape of the nameplate. Follow the edges of the design using the X-Y Axis Joystick. Ensure the spindle cuts through the plywood without damaging the aluminum bed below.

4

Finish and Decorate

Turn off the spindle and vacuum, then open the lid. Remove the clamps and take out the plywood. Sand the edges of the nameplate for a smooth finish. Optionally, paint or decorate the engraved text and design for a personalized touch.
